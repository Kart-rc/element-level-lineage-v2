How the sizing was done

Not single guesses: every workstream was estimated three independent ways and reconciled to a range. Each range already discounts for real-world focus (~3.5 productive days/week), adds a premium on critical-path integration work, and re-baselines after the Phase 0 slice — so the numbers tighten as evidence arrives.

AI productivity is priced in — conservatively. Ranges already discount the AI-amenable work (boilerplate collectors, schema mapping, tests) but not the judgment- and integration-bound work — which is why adding tooling or headcount to the critical path barely moves the calendar.

05Team capacity

How many teams do we actually need, and for how long? The recommendation below is the whole answer.

Recommendation Two standing teams, running the whole 24 months — not one team per phase.

Both teams are staffed on day one and stay funded for the full horizon. They run in parallel, not in a relay — the phases describe where each team's effort is pointed, not a hand-off from one team to the next. The mix within a team shifts by phase (more platform early, more product later), but headcount stays flat.
