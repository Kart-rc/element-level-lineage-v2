Vertical-slice onboarding estimator

A repeatable way to quote onboarding time for end-to-end, element-level lineage on a high-criticality vertical slice. It decomposes the slice into component archetypes, maps each to the collectors it needs (SCA+LLM, OTel, Spark, Dask, streaming), applies calibrated effort baselines with a connector-reuse learning curve, then adjusts for criticality and complexity — so the team can give a defensible estimate, not a guess.

01The approach — six steps

Estimate bottom-up from components, not top-down from a gut feel. Each step is measurable and calibrates against real onboardings.

The first component of each collector type pays to stand up that connector; every later component of the same type reuses it — which is why a 2nd streaming service costs a fraction of the 1st. That learning curve is the single biggest lever on the number.

With OTel column-level decorators, streaming shrinks — but doesn't disappear
When OTel uses runtime decorators to extract column-level attributes, the per-service field mapping at the producer and consumer rides on that same mechanism — so it's charged on the OTel line (which is why OTel here carries the heavier baseline). What decorators cannot do is join a producer's event fields to a consumer's across the async boundary: consumer groups, replay and late delivery break the single-trace assumption, so the edge is stitched on the event contract, not the trace. And because decorators only fire on executed paths, a critical app still needs the schema-registry join for completeness. That residual — cross-topic stitching + registry — is the Streaming (Δ) line, now the lightest of the runtime collectors. Drop it entirely and you silently assume decorators stitch producer→consumer for free; they don't.
